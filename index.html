Dr. John Doe, Ph.D. is a licensed clinical psychologist with extensive experience in clinical and forensic settings. He owns and practices at a private therapy office in Los Angeles, where he sees adolescents, adults, older adults, and couples.

How I work

My approach is integrative. I draw most often from psychodynamic, humanistic, and family systems traditions, with behavioral and Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT) tools woven in where they’re useful. What that looks like in the room is a conversation that takes its time — we move slowly enough to notice what is actually happening, and structured enough that you leave with something to keep working with.

The aim of therapy with me isn’t to fix or label you. It is to understand the patterns that have shaped how you move through the world, and to loosen the ones that no longer serve you. Some shifts happen quickly. Others take years. Both are part of the work.

Beyond private practice

Alongside his private practice, Dr. Doe works in California Correctional Health Care Services at Wasco State Prison as part of the Crisis Intervention Team — responding alongside custody, medical, and mental-health staff to inpatient and outpatient psychiatric crises. He conducts comprehensive mental-health evaluations to inform housing, security-level, and treatment placement decisions, including suicide risk assessments and assessment of threats of harm to self or others.

That work informs the private practice in two ways: a comfort with intensity, and a deep respect for how much courage it takes to ask for help.

03 · Specialties

The work we might do together.

I see clients across a wide range of concerns. The themes below are the ones I return to most often — but if what you carry isn’t named here, write to me anyway.

  1. 01

    Trauma & Post-Traumatic Stress

    Trauma lives in the body and in the patterns of relating that formed around it. We move at a pace your nervous system can tolerate — building safety first, then turning toward what happened, then making room for who you are now. Drawing on psychodynamic, somatic, and ACT-informed work.

    PTSD · Complex Trauma · Childhood adversity · Medical trauma
  2. 02

    Grief & Loss

    The death of someone we love, but also the quieter losses — of a marriage, a parent who wasn’t able to be one, of a body or a future that didn’t turn out the way you thought. Grief is not a problem to solve. It’s a relationship to learn to carry.

    Bereavement · Anticipatory grief · Ambiguous loss
  3. 03

    Life Transitions

    Becoming a parent, leaving one. Career pivots, retirement, divorce, the slow private questions that arrive in your forties and again in your sixties. Transitions are where the old story stops working and a new one hasn’t arrived yet. Therapy is one place to sit with that in-between.

    Career & identity · Parenthood · Retirement · Geographic moves
  4. 04

    Relationships — Family, Friends, Co-workers

    The patterns that show up at work, at the dinner table, and in your closest friendships often share a common architecture. We look at how you learned to relate, what costs that has, and what it might look like to ask for what you actually need.

    Family of origin · Workplace dynamics · Attachment patterns
  5. 05

    Marriage & Partnerships

    For couples in any stage — newly serious, decades in, considering an ending, rebuilding after rupture. We slow the conversations that keep repeating, and we work toward repair you can both live with. LGBTQIA+ and non-traditional structures welcome.

    Communication · Repair after rupture · Pre-marital · Discernment
  6. 06

    Forensic & Assessment Consultation

    Drawing on my work with California Correctional Health Care Services, I offer consultation on comprehensive mental-health evaluations, risk assessment, and crisis intervention for institutions and treatment providers. Inquiries welcome.

    Risk assessment · Suicide risk · Crisis response

04 · Services

How sessions work.

Two formats, multiple therapeutic traditions, and a fairly simple shared aim: that you leave the work feeling more like yourself.

01

Individual Therapy

50 minutes · Weekly · In-person or online

Weekly individual therapy for adolescents (13–17), young adults, adults, and older adults (65+). Sessions are 50 minutes and most clients meet once a week, with the option of twice-weekly depth work where it serves the clinical picture.

This is the format most clients come for. Whether you’re bringing a specific concern or a more diffuse sense that something needs attention, we build a working relationship that can hold both.

02

Couples Therapy

60 minutes · Weekly or biweekly · In-person preferred

For partners of any structure or stage. We work to slow the conversations that keep repeating, name the patterns underneath, and make space for repair — not by rehearsing the right words, but by changing the ground you’re standing on when you say them.

Pre-marital, discernment, recovery from rupture, and long-term maintenance work all welcome. LGBTQIA+ and non-traditional partnership structures affirmed.

Therapeutic approaches

The traditions I draw from.

I don’t practice from a single manual. Each of these traditions is a lens, and I use whichever one helps us see what we’re looking at most clearly.

Psychodynamic Therapy

A depth-oriented approach that takes seriously what is unconscious — patterns, defenses, the relationships of your early life. We make the implicit explicit so it can change.

Humanistic Therapy

Rooted in the belief that people grow in the presence of an honest, warm, and accurate relationship. Less about technique, more about the quality of the contact.

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy

ACT helps you move toward what matters even while difficult thoughts and feelings are present. Practical, values-led, and well-suited to anxiety and avoidance.

Family Systems

The self you became was built inside a family. Family-systems work helps us see the role you played, the rules you absorbed, and what you’re free to update now.

06 · FAQ

Common questions.

A short list of the questions clients ask me before our first session. If yours isn’t here, the consultation is a good place to ask it.

What happens in a 15-minute consultation?

It’s a short phone or video call where you share what’s bringing you to therapy and I share how I work. We see if it feels like a fit. There’s no commitment — if I’m not the right person, I’ll do my best to point you toward someone who is.

How often do you meet with clients?

Most clients meet once a week, which I find is the floor for the work to have continuity. Some clients move to twice a week for depth work; others taper to every other week during stable maintenance phases.

How long does therapy take?

It depends on what you’re bringing. Short-term work around a specific concern can be a season — three to six months. Longer-term depth or relational work often runs a year or more. We talk about this openly, and you’re always the one deciding how long you stay.

Do you offer online sessions?

Yes. Sessions are available in-person in Los Angeles and online to anyone located in California at the time of the session, via a secure HIPAA-compliant video platform.

What if I’ve never been in therapy before?

Many of my clients are new to therapy. There’s nothing you need to prepare or research ahead of time. The first few sessions are mostly about getting to know each other, and I’ll walk you through how it works as we go.

What’s your cancellation policy?

Sessions cancelled with less than 24 hours’ notice are billed at the full session fee. Life happens — please be in touch and we’ll work it out together.

Is what I share confidential?

Yes. The work is confidential within the legal and ethical limits of my license — those exceptions (imminent risk of harm, suspected abuse of a vulnerable person, court order) are spelled out in the intake paperwork and we’ll talk through them in our first session.

Do you prescribe medication?

I do not — as a clinical psychologist I provide psychotherapy and assessment but not medication management. If medication becomes part of the conversation, I coordinate with your prescribing provider or help you find one.
